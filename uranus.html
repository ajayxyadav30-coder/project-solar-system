URANUS

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and the third largest planet by diameter. It is classified as an **Ice Giant** (along with Neptune), meaning it consists mainly of water, methane, and ammonia ices above a small rocky core. It was the first planet discovered with the aid of a telescope in 1781 by William Herschel.

Uranus has a distinctive **cyan-colored** appearance, which comes from the methane gas in its atmosphere. The methane absorbs red light and reflects the blue and green portions back to space. Its atmosphere, composed primarily of hydrogen and helium, holds the lowest minimum temperature of any planet in the solar system [Image of Uranus] .

üîÑ The Sideways Spinner

The most unique feature of Uranus is its extreme **axial tilt of 97.77 degrees**. This causes the planet to effectively spin on its side, orbiting the Sun like a rolling ball. Scientists believe this severe tilt may be the result of a collision with an Earth-sized object early in its history.

  • **Extreme Seasons:** This unique tilt leads to the most extreme seasons in the solar system. One pole faces the Sun for about 42 Earth years of continuous sunlight, followed by 42 years of continuous darkness.
  • **Retrograde Rotation:** Like Venus, Uranus rotates backward (retrograde) relative to its orbit, though its unusual tilt makes this less apparent.

Uranus has only been visited once by a spacecraft: **NASA's Voyager 2** in January 1986. This flyby provided almost all of the detailed data we have on the planet, its moons (which are named after characters from the works of William Shakespeare and Alexander Pope), and its unusual magnetic field.
